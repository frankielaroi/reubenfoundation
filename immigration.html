Immigration and Diaspora Support

Protecting, guiding, and empowering Africans and people of African descent worldwide through lawful migration and diaspora integration.

The Immigration and Diaspora Support Department of Reuben Foundation Africa exists to protect, guide, and empower Africans and people of African descent who are living, studying, working, or seeking legal migration opportunities outside their home countries. The Department provides trusted immigration guidance, legal support access, diaspora integration services, and migrant protection across Africa, North America, Europe, the Middle East, and other regions.

Our mission is to ensure that no African migrant is left without information, protection, or opportunity because of lack of resources, legal support, or guidance.

Our Core Objectives

  • To provide safe, lawful, and accessible immigration support to Africans worldwide
  • To protect the rights and dignity of migrants and international students
  • To support legal work, study, family, and humanitarian migration pathways
  • To strengthen African diaspora integration and economic inclusion
  • To advocate for fair and humane migration policies
  • To build stronger links between Africa and its global diaspora

Who We Serve

  • Undocumented migrants seeking legal pathways
  • International students and graduates abroad
  • Skilled workers and job seekers
  • Families seeking reunification
  • Refugees and humanitarian migrants
  • Vulnerable migrants at risk of detention or deportation
  • African diaspora communities worldwide

Our Key Service Units

  1. Legal and Documentation Support Unit

    We provide access to reliable immigration guidance and connect clients to licensed immigration lawyers through our global legal network.

    Services include:

    • Visa and residency guidance
    • Work permits and employment based migration support
    • Student visa and study pathway assistance
    • Family reunification case support
    • Asylum and humanitarian case referrals
    • Deportation defense referrals
    • Appeals and legal documentation support

    Initial consultations and case intake support are free through the Foundation. Where legal representation requires fees, we work with clients through direct payment, subsidies, or deferred payment agreements based on eligibility.

  2. International Students and Youth Mobility Unit

    This unit supports African students and young professionals navigating international education and mobility challenges.

    Services include:

    • Student visa guidance and compliance support
    • Study permit renewals and transitions
    • Emergency student welfare support
    • Housing and settlement referrals
    • Mental health and wellbeing referrals
    • Career guidance for graduates transitioning into work permits

  3. Work and Skilled Migration Unit

    We support Africans seeking lawful employment opportunities abroad.

    Services include:

    • Work permit and employer sponsored visa guidance
    • Skilled migration pathway advisory
    • Job readiness support
    • Employer and recruitment partner referrals
    • Rights education for migrant workers
    • Workplace exploitation reporting and referral

  4. Family Reunification and Humanitarian Support Unit

    This unit supports migrants seeking protection for themselves and their families.

    Services include:

    • Family reunification application support
    • Refugee and humanitarian pathway guidance
    • Protection services for vulnerable migrants
    • Emergency referrals during detention or forced removal threats
    • Partnership based shelter and welfare referrals

  5. Diaspora Integration and Settlement Support Unit

    We help migrants successfully integrate into their new countries while remaining connected to Africa.

    Services include:

    • Cultural orientation and settlement guidance
    • Financial literacy and community education
    • Diaspora mentorship and peer support
    • Community networking and youth leadership programs
    • Mental health and trauma support referrals

  6. Migration Rights and Policy Advocacy Unit

    This unit works closely with the Social Change Department to protect migrant rights and influence fair migration systems.

    Activities include:

    • Migrant rights education
    • Anti discrimination campaigns
    • Policy research and advocacy
    • Community documentation of migrant abuses
    • Engagement with policymakers and civil society

Eligibility Policy

Reuben Foundation's Immigration and Diaspora Support services are primarily available to:

  • Africans and people of African descent worldwide
  • International students, workers, families, refugees, and vulnerable migrants
  • Both documented and undocumented individuals seeking lawful immigration solutions

Support is provided strictly through legal and ethical migration pathways. Vulnerable clients may qualify for subsidized or deferred legal fee support based on need.

We do not support:

  • Fraudulent applications
  • False documentation
  • Illegal migration activities
  • Human trafficking or smuggling

All clients must provide truthful information and agree to our service and conduct guidelines. Reuben Foundation reserves the right to decline cases outside its mission or capacity.

How Our Support Works

  1. Initial Contact: The client contacts Reuben Foundation Africa through our website, helpline, or partner organizations.
  2. Case Assessment: Our trained officers conduct free initial screening and guidance.
  3. Legal Referral: Where legal representation is required, the case is referred to our accredited partner lawyers.
  4. Support and Follow Up: We monitor case progress while providing welfare and integration support where needed.
  5. Outcome and Reintegration: Clients receive continued guidance after their immigration outcome for stability and compliance.

Our Legal Support Funding Model

We operate a flexible and humane support model:

  • Free initial consultation and guidance
  • Direct payment to lawyers by clients where possible
  • Deferred legal fee support for qualified vulnerable clients
  • Partial or full legal fee subsidies using donor funds
  • Structured repayment agreements after successful outcomes where applicable

Global Reach

The Immigration and Diaspora Support Department operates through:

  • A central coordination office under Reuben Foundation Africa
  • Regional volunteer country leads
  • Partner law firms and advocacy groups
  • Diaspora community focal points in key cities worldwide

Partnerships

We collaborate with:

  • Licensed immigration law firms
  • Universities and international education institutions
  • Employers and recruitment agencies
  • Mental health and social welfare professionals
  • Diaspora community organizations
  • Embassies and international agencies where applicable

Why Choose Reuben Foundation Africa

  • Trusted African led institution
  • Client centered and confidential services
  • Strong legal and advocacy partnerships
  • Ethical and transparent operations
  • Deep understanding of African migration realities
  • Integrated social, legal, and humanitarian support

Our Commitment

Reuben Foundation Africa is committed to ensuring that migration becomes a pathway to dignity, not exploitation. We do not encourage illegal migration. All our services promote lawful migration, human rights protection, and sustainable integration.
