Program Overview

The Reuben Foundation Africa Scholarship & Mentorship Program is designed to support high-potential undergraduate students studying in Africa who face financial barriers in accessing quality education. We provide partial or full scholarships to cover tuition, academic fees, and essential learning materials. In some cases, limited support for accommodation or living expenses may also be considered.

What makes this program unique is its holistic approach. In addition to financial assistance, each selected student is paired with a mentor, a professional, academic, or industry leader who offers personalized guidance, career mentorship, and emotional support throughout their academic journey.

The program also encourages students with a strong interest in research that contributes to Africa's development. We believe that ideas born in classrooms can drive real-world change, and we want to nurture students who aspire to innovate, analyze, and solve pressing issues across the continent.

Program Objectives

Our mission is to create equal access to opportunity for deserving students who have the talent but not the means. Through this program, we aim to:

Bridge Financial Gaps

Bridge the financial gap for talented African students in need

Leadership Development

Provide mentorship and leadership coaching for long-term success

Scholar Network

Foster a network of scholars committed to driving transformation in Africa

Research & Innovation

Encourage students to engage in meaningful research that addresses Africa's challenges

Ethical Leadership

Promote ethical leadership, innovation, and social responsibility

Eligibility Criteria

Must be an undergraduate student currently enrolled in a recognized African tertiary institution

Demonstrate academic excellence and leadership potential

Come from an underserved or low-income background

Have a clear interest in making a positive impact in Africa through research, leadership, or innovation

Must not currently hold a full scholarship from another source

Special Research Focus

We strongly encourage applications from students who:

Research Areas

Plan to conduct research on topics related to sustainable development, public policy, agriculture, health, education, climate, or technology in Africa

Innovation Focus

Have ideas that could be transformed into real solutions, community projects, or startups

Collaboration Opportunities

Are eager to collaborate with the Reuben Foundation Research Fund or future incubator opportunities

How the Mentorship Works

Each selected student is matched with a mentor based on their academic discipline or career interests. The mentorship includes:

Regular Check-ins

Monthly one-on-one check-ins (virtual or in-person)

Holistic Guidance

Guidance on academic, career, and personal development

Networking

Access to leadership workshops and networking opportunities

Research Support

Feedback and encouragement for any research or innovation ideas

Student Responsibilities

Award recipients must commit to:

Maintaining satisfactory academic performance (minimum GPA requirements apply)

Participating fully in mentorship sessions and foundation-led activities

Submitting semester progress and feedback reports

Serving as ambassadors in their universities or communities

Engaging in one volunteer or community development initiative each year
