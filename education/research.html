Reuben Foundation Research Fund

Empowering African Innovation for Global Impact

Apply Now Learn More

Overview

The Reuben Foundation Research Fund is a transformative, pan-African initiative committed to supporting bold, research-based innovations developed by African students, graduates, and emerging changemakers. We focus on turning academic research and technical ideas into real-world impact by providing early-stage funding, mentorship, and professional support.

Across Africa, thousands of students complete powerful research projects every year—many tackling critical challenges in health, agriculture, energy, technology, and more. Yet, due to a lack of funding, exposure, and technical backing, much of this work is shelved, forgotten, or never commercialized. This Fund exists to change that.

Whether you're building a healthtech app, designing a clean-energy prototype, developing a new AgriTech tool, or leading a social innovation, we are here to help you turn your research into a solution and your idea into an innovation that can create jobs, build businesses, and solve real problems across Africa.

Our Mission

To unlock the transformative potential of African research by bridging the gap between knowledge and implementation. We aim to build a new generation of African innovators, equipped not just with ideas, but with the resources, mentorship, and platforms to bring them to life.

"Africa's future will not only be imported but it will be invented. And much of it lies in the hands of young Africans ready to build."

Who Can Apply

We welcome applications from a diverse group of young African innovators, including:

Tertiary Students

  • Undergraduate and postgraduate students (Master's, MPhil, PhD)
  • Enrolled in African universities, polytechnics, or accredited technical institutions

Recent Graduates

  • Graduated within the last 2 years from any recognized tertiary institution in Africa
  • If beyond 2 years: the project must have originated during or immediately after studies and must still be uncommercialized with strong potential

Self-Taught or Non-Tertiary Innovators

  • Must demonstrate strong subject expertise and hands-on experience
  • Idea must be research-based and practically tested
  • Must have an advisory or technical mentor involved in the project

Application Requirements

To apply, each applicant or team must submit the following:

1

Research Proposal Summary (max 3 pages)

  • • Clearly describe the problem, your innovation, and its potential impact
  • • Show how your idea could evolve into a solution, product, or venture
2

Proof of Enrollment or Graduation

(optional but preferred)

3

CV or Short Profile

Of the lead applicant or team

4

Early-Stage Feasibility or Implementation Plan

5

Recommendation Letter

From a supervisor, academic mentor, or professional (optional but encouraged)

6

Supporting Documents

  • • Diagrams or sketches
  • • Prototype photos or demo videos
  • • Survey data or early test results

Evaluation Criteria

Your application will be reviewed by a panel of experts based on the following:

Innovation & Originality

Uniqueness and creativity of the concept

Relevance to African Development Goals

Alignment with Africa's development priorities

Potential for Scalable Impact

Ability to create widespread positive change

Technical & Financial Feasibility

Viability and sustainability of the solution

Passion, Leadership & Commitment

Dedication and drive of the applicant(s) to see the project through

Application Timeline

The Reuben Foundation Research Fund opens for applications once every year.

Opening Date: January 1

Applications open for submission

Deadline: January 31

Final date for application submission

Shortlisting: February

Initial review and shortlisting of applications

Interviews & Selection: March

Final interviews and selection process

Grants Disbursed: April

Successful applicants receive funding and support

Important: Late submissions will not be accepted. Shortlisted applicants may be contacted for further interviews or presentations.
