Privacy Policy

Your privacy is important to us. Learn how we protect and handle your personal information.

Effective Date: January 1, 2025

At Reuben Foundation Africa ("we," "our," or "us"), your privacy is important to us. We are committed to protecting the personal information you share with us and ensuring transparency in how that information is collected, used, and safeguarded.

This Privacy Policy explains our practices regarding the collection, use, storage, and protection of your data when you:

  • Visit our official website (www.reubenfoundation.africa)
  • Apply for scholarships, grants, or other support programs
  • Make donations or contributions
  • Subscribe to our newsletters or updates
  • Participate in our campaigns, surveys, or initiatives
  • Contact us or engage with our services through online platforms

By accessing our website or submitting any personal information through our programs, forms, or communication channels, you agree to the terms outlined in this policy. If you do not agree with this policy, please do not use our website or submit your personal information.

We encourage you to read this policy carefully and contact us if you have any questions or concerns about how your data is handled.

1. Information We Collect

We may collect both personal and non-personal information from you depending on how you interact with our website or participate in our programs and services.

a. Personal Information

This refers to any information that can be used to identify you. We collect personal information when you voluntarily provide it to us through forms, applications, registrations, donations, or communication. This may include:

  • Full name
  • Email address
  • Phone number
  • Country of residence
  • Educational or professional background
  • Date of birth (where applicable)
  • Identification documents (for grant or scholarship verification)
  • Payment or donation details (processed securely through third-party services)

b. Non-Personal Information

We may also collect data that does not identify you directly, such as:

  • Browser type and version
  • Device type and operating system
  • IP address and general location
  • Pages visited and duration on site
  • Referring URLs and site navigation patterns

This information is collected automatically via cookies, analytics tools, or server logs and is used to help us improve the performance and user experience of our website.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to serve you better and fulfill our mission across Africa. Your personal and non-personal data may be used for the following purposes:

a. Program Administration

  • To review and process applications for scholarships, grants, or support programs
  • To communicate decisions and follow up on selected beneficiaries
  • To verify eligibility and prevent fraudulent submissions

b. Donor Engagement and Fundraising

  • To process donations and issue receipts
  • To keep donors informed about how their contributions are being used
  • To invite ongoing support or participation in future campaigns

c. Communication and Updates

  • To send newsletters, updates, invitations, or announcements about our programs
  • To respond to inquiries or requests submitted through the website or by email
  • To collect feedback to improve our services

d. Website Improvement and Analytics

  • To monitor traffic patterns, user behavior, and technical performance
  • To personalize user experience based on preferences and activity
  • To diagnose technical issues and maintain website security

e. Legal and Administrative Compliance

  • To comply with legal obligations or enforce policies
  • To protect the rights, safety, and property of Reuben Foundation Africa, its users, and partners

We do not sell, rent, or lease your personal information to third parties for commercial purposes.

3. How We Share Your Information

We are committed to protecting your privacy and will only share your personal information when it is necessary, lawful, and aligned with the purpose for which it was collected.

a. With Service Providers and Partners

We may share your information with trusted third-party service providers who help us deliver our programs, operate our website, or carry out administrative functions. These providers include payment processors, application reviewers, hosting services, email communication platforms, and data management tools. All service providers are required to handle your data securely and are only permitted to use it for the specific tasks assigned to them by us.

b. With Government or Legal Authorities

We may disclose your personal information if required to do so by law, regulation, legal process, or government request. This may include court orders, audits, or lawful investigations. We will only do so when such disclosure is necessary to comply with our legal obligations or to protect the rights, property, or safety of Reuben Foundation Africa, our users, or the public.

c. With Your Consent

In some cases, we may ask for your explicit permission to share your information with a third party. For example, if you are selected for a scholarship or grant administered in partnership with another organization, we may request your consent to forward your details to that partner for processing and verification.

d. In the Event of a Structural Change

If Reuben Foundation Africa undergoes a merger, acquisition, reorganization, or asset transfer, your personal information may be transferred to the new entity as part of the transition. In such an event, we will ensure that your data remains protected and that you are informed of any significant changes that affect your rights or the handling of your information.

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information for marketing or commercial resale purposes under any circumstances.

4. Data Security and Storage

We take the security of your personal information seriously and implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect it from unauthorized access, loss, misuse, alteration, or destruction.

a. Security Measures

We use industry-standard security protocols to safeguard data both in transit and at rest. This includes the use of:

  • Secure Sockets Layer (SSL) encryption to protect data during transmission
  • Firewalls and regular system monitoring
  • Password-protected databases with restricted access
  • Secure servers maintained by trusted hosting providers

Access to your personal data is limited to authorized staff, volunteers, or third-party service providers who need it to perform specific tasks related to our programs, services, or operations. These individuals and entities are required to maintain the confidentiality and security of your information.

b. Data Retention

We retain your personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purpose for which it was collected. This includes fulfilling legal, accounting, or reporting requirements. When your information is no longer needed, we securely delete or anonymize it.

For example:

  • Scholarship and grant applicants' information may be stored until the end of the program cycle or in line with donor reporting obligations
  • Donor and financial records may be kept for a period required by tax or legal regulations

c. Limitations

While we do our best to protect your information, no method of electronic storage or transmission over the internet is completely secure. Therefore, we cannot guarantee absolute security, but we will act swiftly and responsibly in the event of a data breach or unauthorized access.

We encourage you to help keep your own data secure by avoiding the use of public networks when submitting personal information and by notifying us immediately if you suspect unauthorized activity related to your data.

5. Your Rights and Choices

We value your trust and want you to feel confident about how your personal information is used. As a user, you have the right to make certain choices and exercise control over the data you share with us.

a. Right to Access

You have the right to request access to the personal information we hold about you. This includes details about how we collected the data, what we use it for, and with whom it has been shared.

b. Right to Correction

If you believe that any of your personal information is inaccurate or incomplete, you have the right to request a correction. We will promptly update your information once verified.

c. Right to Deletion

You may request that we delete your personal data from our systems, subject to any legal or operational obligations that require us to retain certain records. Once verified, we will remove your data from our active systems.

d. Right to Withdraw Consent

If we are processing your personal data based on your consent, you have the right to withdraw that consent at any time. This may affect your access to certain services or programs.

e. Right to Object or Restrict Processing

You can object to the processing of your personal data for certain purposes, or request that we limit the way we use your data. For example, you may ask us not to send you newsletters or marketing communications.

f. Managing Communication Preferences

You can opt in or out of email updates, newsletters, and other non-essential communications. Every promotional email we send includes an unsubscribe link you can use at any time.

Exercising Your Rights

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at:

Email: info@reubenfoundation.africa
Subject Line: Data Request – [Your Name]

We may require proof of identity before processing your request. We aim to respond to all data-related inquiries within a reasonable time frame.

6. Children's Privacy

Reuben Foundation Africa is committed to safeguarding the privacy and rights of children and minors who may visit our website or participate in our programs. We recognize the importance of protecting children's personal information and comply with all applicable laws and ethical standards in doing so.

a. Collection of Information from Children

Our website and programs are not primarily directed at children under the age of 18. However, some of our initiatives, such as educational scholarships, youth development programs, or creative arts competitions, may involve minors as participants. In such cases, we may collect limited personal information (such as name, age, contact information, and academic history) for the sole purpose of administering the program.

We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under the age of 18 without the express consent of a parent or legal guardian. If it is discovered that such information has been collected without appropriate consent, we will take prompt steps to delete it from our systems.

b. Parental or Guardian Consent

For any program or service where children under the age of 18 are involved, we require:

  • Verifiable parental or guardian consent before collecting or processing any personal information
  • Clear explanation of how the data will be used
  • Assurance that consent can be withdrawn at any time

We do not use children's personal data for marketing or commercial purposes.

c. Rights of Parents and Guardians

Parents or legal guardians have the following rights regarding the personal data of their children:

  • Access: You may request to see the information we have collected about your child
  • Correction: You may request updates or changes to incorrect or outdated information
  • Deletion: You may request that we delete your child's personal information from our records

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at info@reubenfoundation.africa with proof of identity and relationship to the child.

d. Special Programs and Educational Platforms

When organizing school-based or youth-targeted programs, we work with teachers, schools, community leaders, and guardians to ensure that all interactions with children are conducted in a safe, respectful, and privacy-conscious manner. Any media or public-facing content involving minors will only be published with written consent from a parent or legal guardian.

e. Compliance

We comply with Ghana's Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843) and other relevant African data protection laws regarding the handling of children's information. We aim to promote a culture of privacy, respect, and safety for all young people who interact with our foundation.

7. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

To enhance your experience and ensure our website functions properly, Reuben Foundation Africa uses cookies and similar tracking technologies. This section explains what cookies are, how they are used on our website, and how you can manage your preferences regarding them.

a. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your computer, smartphone, tablet, or other device when you visit a website. These files contain data that help websites recognize returning users, remember user preferences, and optimize functionality.

There are two main categories of cookies:

  • Session cookies: These are temporary cookies that are erased when you close your browser. They are used to remember your activities during a single browsing session.
  • Persistent cookies: These cookies remain stored on your device even after you close your browser. They are used to remember you when you return to the website and may remain until you delete them manually or they expire.

b. Why We Use Cookies

Reuben Foundation Africa uses cookies for several important purposes, including:

  • Website functionality: To remember your selections, such as language, location, or previously entered form data. This helps make future visits more convenient and efficient.
  • Analytics and performance monitoring: To understand how visitors interact with our site, such as which pages are viewed most often, how long users stay on each page, and what type of devices are used to access the site. This data helps us improve content, layout, and navigation.
  • Security: To detect irregular or potentially malicious behavior, such as repeated login attempts or unusual traffic patterns. Cookies help us protect your data and ensure a safe browsing experience.
  • Page speed and optimization: To assist with faster loading of web pages and to deliver a smoother user experience by remembering settings and layout preferences.

c. Third-Party Cookies

We may allow carefully selected third-party services to place cookies on your device for purposes such as website analytics and embedded tools. For example:

  • Google Analytics may collect anonymous statistics on page visits, traffic sources, and user behavior to help us measure and improve our site performance.
  • YouTube, social media platforms, or embedded maps may set cookies if you play a video, share content, or view interactive tools on our pages.

These third parties do not receive your personal data unless you explicitly provide it. We ensure that all third-party services used are compliant with relevant data protection laws.

d. Your Cookie Choices

You have control over how cookies are used and stored on your device. Here are your main options:

  • Browser settings: Most web browsers (such as Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge) allow you to adjust cookie settings. You can block or restrict cookies, delete existing cookies, or set your browser to notify you before a cookie is placed.
  • Opt-out options: You can choose to opt out of specific cookies used for analytics, such as by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on, which prevents your data from being used by Google Analytics.
  • Disable third-party cookies: Some browsers also allow you to disable third-party cookies while still allowing first-party cookies that are essential to the website's function.

Please note that if you disable or delete cookies, certain features of our website may not function correctly. This could include remembering your login status, preferred language, or completed forms.

e. Consent

By continuing to browse or use our website, you agree to the use of cookies and similar technologies as described in this Privacy Policy. On your first visit, you may be presented with a cookie banner or notice that allows you to accept or manage your cookie preferences.

You may update or change your cookie preferences at any time by adjusting your browser settings or clearing your stored cookies.

8. Data Security

Reuben Foundation Africa is fully committed to protecting the integrity, confidentiality, and security of your personal information. We implement a wide range of administrative, technical, and physical safeguards designed to protect the data you share with us from unauthorized access, misuse, loss, alteration, or destruction.

a. Technical Security Measures

We use industry-standard technologies to secure data both in transit and at rest. These measures include:

  • Secure Socket Layer (SSL) Encryption: Our website is secured with SSL encryption, which ensures that any information transmitted between your device and our servers is encrypted and protected from interception by unauthorized parties.
  • Firewall Protection: We maintain up-to-date firewall systems to shield our network and servers from external threats, intrusion attempts, or malicious traffic.
  • Antivirus and Malware Protection: Our systems are protected with antivirus and anti-malware solutions that are regularly updated to detect and block known threats.
  • Regular Security Patching: We update all software, plugins, and server components frequently to fix vulnerabilities and reduce the risk of cyberattacks.

b. Administrative Safeguards

  • Access Control: Access to personal data is limited to authorized personnel or service providers who need the information to perform specific tasks, such as processing applications, managing donations, or responding to inquiries.
  • Confidentiality Agreements: Staff and third-party partners are required to sign confidentiality and data protection agreements, ensuring they handle your data responsibly and within legal boundaries.
  • Staff Training: Our team receives ongoing training in data protection principles, cybersecurity awareness, and proper handling of sensitive information.

c. Physical Security

  • Secure Facilities: Any physical records or servers we use are kept in secure locations with restricted access.
  • Data Disposal: We dispose of outdated paper records or obsolete digital files securely through shredding or certified data erasure to prevent unauthorized retrieval.

d. Third-Party Service Providers

When we engage trusted third-party vendors to process data on our behalf (such as cloud storage providers, donation platforms, or analytics services), we ensure that they are compliant with international data protection standards. We review their security practices, require data processing agreements, and monitor their performance to protect your information.

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal information with any third parties for marketing purposes.

e. User Responsibility and Caution

While we do everything reasonably possible to protect your data, online systems can never be fully immune to security risks. You are encouraged to take the following precautions:

  • Use secure and private internet connections when submitting information through our website.
  • Avoid sharing your personal login credentials with others.
  • Log out from your account after using our platforms, especially on shared or public devices.
  • Be cautious of unsolicited emails, messages, or links claiming to represent Reuben Foundation Africa.

If you suspect that your personal information has been compromised through our website or suspect any suspicious activity, please notify us immediately at info@reubenfoundation.africa so we can take swift action.

9. Your Rights and Choices

At Reuben Foundation Africa, we believe that you have full control over how your personal information is collected, used, and shared. We respect your rights and are committed to making it easy for you to exercise them. This section explains the choices available to you and the rights you may have under applicable data protection laws, including the Ghana Data Protection Act, the African Union Convention on Cybersecurity and Personal Data Protection, and international privacy regulations where applicable.

a. Right to Access Your Information

You have the right to request access to the personal data we hold about you. This includes:

  • The types of personal information we collect
  • The purposes for which it is used
  • The third parties we may share it with
  • The duration we intend to retain it

You may make an access request at any time by contacting us at info@reubenfoundation.africa. We will respond within a reasonable period, subject to verifying your identity.

b. Right to Correction or Rectification

If you believe any information we hold about you is inaccurate, incomplete, or outdated, you have the right to request that it be corrected or updated. We will make the necessary corrections promptly once your identity is verified.

c. Right to Erasure (Also Known as the Right to Be Forgotten)

You may request that we delete your personal information from our records, especially if:

  • The data is no longer necessary for the purpose it was collected
  • You withdraw your consent
  • You object to the processing and there are no overriding legitimate grounds

However, we may need to retain some data where legally required or for legitimate operational needs, such as financial or audit obligations.

d. Right to Object to Processing

You have the right to object to the processing of your personal data for certain purposes, including:

  • Direct marketing
  • Profiling
  • Processing based on legitimate interests that you believe affect your rights and freedoms

If you raise an objection, we will stop processing your information unless we have compelling reasons to continue.

e. Right to Restrict Processing

In some situations, you may ask us to temporarily restrict how we use your information, for example:

  • If you dispute the accuracy of the data and want it verified
  • If the data is being used unlawfully but you do not want it deleted
  • If you have objected to processing and are awaiting a decision

During the restriction period, your data will not be used for any other purpose without your permission.

f. Right to Data Portability

If applicable, you may request to receive a digital copy of the personal data you have provided to us in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format. You can also ask us to transfer that data directly to another organization, where technically feasible.

g. Right to Withdraw Consent

Where your personal data is processed based on your consent, such as when you sign up for newsletters or events, you may withdraw that consent at any time. Withdrawing consent will not affect the lawfulness of any processing done before the withdrawal.

To withdraw your consent, simply:

  • Click the "unsubscribe" link in any of our emails
  • Email us directly at info@reubenfoundation.africa

h. Communication Preferences

You can choose how you would like to hear from us. For example, you may:

  • Subscribe only to specific updates (such as scholarships or health programs)
  • Opt out of receiving promotional or fundraising messages
  • Choose to receive information via email, SMS, or both

We respect your preferences and will update them upon request.

How to Exercise These Rights

To exercise any of the rights listed above, please contact us by email at:

Email: info@reubenfoundation.africa
Subject Line: Data Rights Request

We may need to verify your identity before processing your request. We aim to respond within 30 days or sooner, depending on applicable regulations.
