Our Mission

The Reuben Foundation Health Access Fund is a compassionate initiative designed to support vulnerable individuals and communities in accessing urgent, essential, and life-improving healthcare services across Africa.

Every year, thousands of people across the continent lose their lives not because treatment doesn't exist but because they cannot afford it, or live too far from it. Our mission is to bridge this gap.

We provide financial support, emergency medical aid, and community-based health interventions that ensure no African is left behind due to poverty, distance, or lack of awareness.

Whether it's helping a child get heart surgery, supporting a rural clinic with medical supplies, or funding a maternal health campaign, this Fund exists to make quality healthcare more accessible, especially in underserved and hard-to-reach areas.

Our Focus Areas

We fund both individual medical aid and grassroots health initiatives under these priority areas:

1

Emergency and Essential Care

Urgent medical procedures, surgeries, and treatments for life-threatening or disabling conditions.

2

Maternal and Child Health

Prenatal care, safe delivery, neonatal support, vaccinations, and nutrition for mothers and babies.

Mental Health Support

Access to counseling, trauma healing, and mental health services, especially for youth and post-conflict communities.

Rural and Mobile Health Outreach

Deployment of nurses, mobile clinics, and health educators to remote or underserved regions.

Medical Transport Assistance

Support for ambulance services, referral travel, and inter-hospital transfers.

Public Health Education and Prevention

Awareness campaigns on hygiene, nutrition, reproductive health, malaria, HIV/AIDS, and community wellness.

Community Health Infrastructure

Small-scale support for rural clinics, diagnostic tools, medical equipment, and health facilities.

Who Can Apply?

We accept applications or referrals from:

Individuals & Families

Low-income individuals or families facing serious health challenges

Organizations & NGOs

Nonprofit organizations, health NGOs, or community-based groups

Healthcare Workers

Doctors, nurses, or social workers at public health institutions

Educational Institutions

University public health programs or student-led health initiatives

Crisis-Affected Communities

Displaced individuals or crisis-affected communities

Eligibility Criteria

Applicants or beneficiaries must:

Reside in an African country

Must be a resident of any African nation

Show financial need or lack of access

Demonstrate inability to afford quality healthcare

Provide medical evidence

Documentation of urgent or essential health needs

For organizations: Clear plan and accountability

Present target impact and accountability structure

Note: For individual medical aid, the patient must be a citizen of an African country. Medical diagnosis and recommended treatment documentation are required.

What We Fund

Hospital admission fees, surgeries, medication, and diagnostics
Cost of medical referrals and transportation (e.g. ambulances)
Deployment of mobile health staff for outreach programs
Procurement of medical equipment for local use
Maternal health campaigns and postnatal care programs
Mental health support services and trauma recovery sessions
Community sensitization, vaccination drives, or disease prevention education

What We Do Not Fund

Cosmetic, elective, or non-essential procedures
Applications without verified diagnosis or treatment recommendation
Salaries not directly related to frontline health service delivery
Recurring administrative costs of private health facilities
Applications lacking clear documentation or with unverifiable claims

1

Medical Documentation

Diagnosis, doctor's report, or hospital invoice

2

Personal Profile or Case Summary

3

Proof of Residence and Valid ID

4

Detailed Budget or Cost Breakdown

For organizations and health initiatives

5

Recommendation Letter

From a doctor, community leader, or medical officer (optional but strongly encouraged)

Review & Disbursement Process

Review Timeline

Rolling Basis

Applications are reviewed throughout the year

Priority Cases

Time-sensitive or life-threatening cases

Pregnant women, children, and persons with disabilities

Rural, displaced, or conflict-affected communities

Health initiatives with high potential for sustainable impact

Important: All disbursements are made directly to licensed medical institutions or service providers. We do not issue personal payments unless under extraordinary and well-verified conditions.

Our Commitment

At Reuben Foundation Africa, we believe that access to healthcare is a right, not a privilege. Through the Health Access Fund, we are committed to building healthier communities, supporting the most vulnerable, and strengthening Africa's health systems one patient, one project, one village at a time.

If you or someone you know is in need of health-related support, we are here to help.

Let's give every African a fighting chance at life.
