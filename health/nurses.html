International Nurse Job Placement Program

Connecting Africa's Nursing Talent to Global Healthcare Opportunities

Apply Now Learn More

Transforming Lives Through Global Opportunities

The Reuben Foundation Africa's International Nurse Job Placement Program is a transformative initiative designed to connect skilled and passionate African nurses to top-tier healthcare systems around the world. Through strategic collaborations with international hospitals, accredited recruiters, and training institutions, this program helps African nurses gain access to high-paying, long-term employment in countries experiencing critical healthcare workforce shortages such as the United States, United Kingdom, Canada, Australia, and other regions.

We believe that Africa's health professionals deserve the same global opportunities as their peers elsewhere. While the continent continues to train highly competent nurses, many remain underemployed due to limited local opportunities, low wages, and constrained resources. At the same time, global health systems are facing acute shortages in qualified nursing staff. This program bridges that gap.

What the Program Offers

The Nurse Job Placement Program is not just a recruitment pipeline. It is a comprehensive, end-to-end support system designed to prepare, guide, and empower nurses every step of the way.

Eligibility Screening and Documentation Support

We assist qualified applicants in understanding international job requirements, gathering needed documentation, and preparing for the application process.

Educational credential evaluation
Transcripts, licenses, and verification processes
Background checks and police clearance assistance
Regulatory compliance with destination country health boards

Exam Preparation Support

Most destination countries require nursing applicants to pass specific exams. We offer structured guidance and preparation assistance for:

NCLEX-RN IELTS OET CBT OSCE

We partner with certified training institutions and online platforms to help nurses access affordable or sponsored test prep programs.

Visa Sponsorship & Immigration Support

Through our recruitment partners, we facilitate:

Work visa sponsorship for eligible nurses and families
Immigration paperwork and embassy procedures
Temporary to permanent residency pathways

Relocation & Settlement Assistance

Moving to a new country can be overwhelming. We ensure every nurse receives help with:

Flight arrangements and airport reception
Temporary accommodation coordination
Medical registration and employment orientation
Cultural integration and community introductions

Post-Arrival Mentoring & Career Guidance

We remain engaged with nurses even after their arrival abroad. Our team and partners provide:

Peer support networks and mentorship
Ongoing career development and continuing education
Mental health check-ins and transition counseling

Who Can Apply?

We welcome applications from:

Registered Nurses

With a valid practicing license from any African country

Recent Graduates

From accredited nursing institutions (who are eligible to register)

Experienced Nurses

With at least one year of clinical experience in hospitals, clinics, or community health

English Proficient

Candidates proficient in English (written and spoken) with passion for patient care

Note: Applicants must meet the requirements set by nursing boards in destination countries. We will guide you through the full process based on your chosen country.

Our Approach to Ethical Recruitment

At Reuben Foundation Africa, we promote ethical migration. Our partnerships are carefully vetted to ensure:

No Exploitation

Nurses are not exploited or overcharged by recruiters

Fair Employment

Employers provide fair wages, humane working conditions, and long-term contracts

Transparency

Participants receive transparent contracts, full information, and pre-departure briefings

Balanced Approach

Local health systems are not depleted through mass recruitment‚Äîwe support balanced, mutually beneficial arrangements

We also encourage reinvestment of skills and knowledge back into African health systems through mentorship, future return opportunities, or remittance-supported community health projects.
