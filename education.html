Education Programs

Empowering Africa's future through knowledge and skills

At Reuben Foundation Africa, we believe education is the foundation for Africa's long-term transformation. Our programs are designed to be practical, inclusive, and forward-thinking, ensuring Africa's youth are equipped with the knowledge and skills needed to thrive in a modern, digital-first economy.

We go beyond traditional education by integrating technology, innovation, and entrepreneurship into every learning experience. Whether in remote villages or urban centers, we strive to bridge the opportunity gap with future-ready education.

Reuben Foundation Research Fund

Empowering African Innovation for Global Impact

The Reuben Foundation Research Fund is a pan-African initiative dedicated to turning bold ideas into real impact. We support research-driven innovations from students, graduates, and young changemakers across Africa who are ready to transform their knowledge into practical, scalable solutions.

We recognize that across the continent, thousands of university students complete groundbreaking research each year, but much of it remains unused, unpublished, or forgotten due to a lack of funding, mentorship, and industry connection. This program exists to change that.

Whether youâ€™re creating a healthtech app, developing a sustainable farming tool, or pioneering a breakthrough in clean energy, our mission is to help you move from concept to implementation. We provide funding, mentorship, and technical support to bring local solutions to life, with the potential to grow into businesses, social ventures, or commercial products that serve Africa and the world.

Our Focus Areas

  • Agriculture and Agribusiness
  • Healthcare and Life Sciences
  • Green and Renewable Energy
  • Digital Innovation and Artificial Intelligence
  • Education and EdTech
  • Financial Technology (Fintech)
  • Manufacturing and Industrial Design
  • Water, Sanitation, and Hygiene (WASH)
  • Climate Change and Environmental Solutions
  • Local production, value chains, and job creation

What We Offer

  • Seed funding or early-stage grant capital
  • Mentorship and business incubation
  • Legal guidance and intellectual property support
  • Prototype development and technical assistance
  • Investor readiness and pitch opportunities
  • Access to labs, expert networks, and global partners

Who Can Apply

  • Tertiary students enrolled in universities, polytechnics, HND, diploma, or accredited technical institutions
  • Postgraduate students (Masterâ€™s, MPhil, PhD)
  • Graduates within 2 years of completing a recognized tertiary program
  • Graduates beyond 2 years, only if:
    • The research was developed during or soon after school
    • It remains uncommercialized and has strong potential
  • Non-tertiary applicants, only if:
    • The idea is research-based and practically tested
    • The applicant demonstrates strong subject knowledge
    • They work with a mentor, advisor, or technical collaborator

Note: While the Fund is primarily designed for students and recent graduates, we also consider exceptional, self-taught innovators with tested ideas and a clear vision for impact.

Application Requirements

  1. Research Summary (maximum 3 pages)
    • Describe the problem you are solving
    • Explain your core idea or innovation
    • Show how it can become a product, service, or business
  2. Proof of enrollment or graduation from an accredited African institution (optional)
  3. CV or brief profile of the lead applicant(s)
  4. Feasibility or implementation plan (can be early-stage)
  5. Recommendation letter from a mentor, supervisor, or professional (optional but encouraged)
  6. Supporting documents, such as photos, test results, sketches, or prototypes (if available)

Evaluation Criteria

  • Innovation and originality
  • Relevance to African context and needs
  • Real-world impact and scalability
  • Technical and practical feasibility
  • Leadership and commitment of the applicant(s)

International Student Loan Support

The Reuben Foundation Africa is committed to expanding access to global education for African students. Through our International Student Loan Support Program, we partner with trusted financial institutions that provide education loans for postgraduate studies in countries such as the US, UK, Canada, and beyond.

This initiative is designed for talented students who meet academic requirements but lack the financial means, collateral, or traditional guarantors required by most lenders. Our role is to bridge that gap by connecting eligible candidates with credible lenders and supporting them through the application process.

The goal is not just to help students study abroad, but to ensure they return with advanced knowledge, global exposure, and a deep commitment to contributing to Africaâ€™s development.

How to Apply

Interested students can apply by filling out the Contact Us form on our website. A member of our team will follow up with guidance and next steps.

Scholarship & Mentorship Program

Our Scholarship and Mentorship Program is designed to support high-potential undergraduate students across Africa who face financial challenges in accessing quality education. We aim to provide partial or full scholarships to cover tuition, academic fees, and essential learning materials.

What makes this program unique is its combination of financial aid and personal development. Each selected student will be paired with a mentor who offers guidance, encouragement, and career support throughout their academic journey. This mentorship component is key to helping students build leadership skills, professional confidence, and long-term vision beyond the classroom.

The program is especially targeted at students from underserved communities who demonstrate academic promise, resilience, and a commitment to making a difference in their local or national context.

This program is not yet open for applications. It will be officially launched soon, and once it is, the application timeline and process will be publicly announced on our website and social media channels.
