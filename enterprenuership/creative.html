Creative Arts Fund

Empowering Africa's Artistic Visionaries

A grant and mentorship program designed to support emerging and established African creatives in transforming their talent into sustainable careers, enterprises, and global platforms.

Apply Now Learn More

Our Mission

Our goal is to nurture the creative economy by investing in individuals and groups who use art to tell powerful African stories, preserve culture, promote social change, and drive economic empowerment.

This fund serves as a bridge between raw creative talent and opportunities for professional growth, production funding, international exposure, and long-term impact.

Key Disciplines We Support

The Creative Arts Fund focuses on four main disciplines that represent the rich tapestry of African artistic expression.

Music

  • Upcoming musicians, producers, and vocalists
  • Music video production and album recording
  • Cultural music preservation and innovation
  • Live performance opportunities
  • Studio time and promotional support

Film & Media

  • Short films and documentaries
  • Animation and storytelling
  • Film equipment and post-production
  • Festival submissions and screenings
  • Independent journalism projects

Dance & Performing Arts

  • Traditional and contemporary dance
  • Theatre productions and performances
  • Choreography training programs
  • Youth dance development
  • Cultural exchange programs

Visual Arts & Design

  • Painters, sculptors, and illustrators
  • Exhibitions and gallery installations
  • Art education in communities
  • Fashion and textile design
  • Digital art and NFTs

What We Offer

Our comprehensive support system goes beyond funding to provide holistic development opportunities.

Creative Grants

Project-based funding ranging from $500 to $5,000, depending on scope and impact.

  • • Production and creative tools
  • • Content distribution and promotion
  • • Venue rental or exhibition costs
  • • Equipment and logistics
  • • Concept development

Mentorship & Coaching

Each grantee is matched with a creative industry mentor for personalized guidance.

  • • Artistic development guidance
  • • Monetization strategies
  • • Contract negotiation
  • • Portfolio building
  • • Branding and audience development

Training & Capacity Building

Comprehensive skill development programs tailored for creative entrepreneurs.

  • • Creative entrepreneurship workshops
  • • Legal and copyright education
  • • Grant writing techniques
  • • Cross-border collaboration
  • • Social media marketing

Showcase Opportunities

Platform your work through various exhibition and performance opportunities.

  • • Creative Arts Festival
  • • Partner exhibitions and tours
  • • Digital campaigns
  • • Media platforms
  • • International collaborations

Who Can Apply?

Emerging or mid-career African creatives

Creative collectives or community-based arts groups

University or tertiary students in creative fields

Individuals with a track record of excellence or innovation

Creatives with strong cultural, social, or economic impact mission

Applicants must be citizens of any African country and demonstrate clear artistic vision, purpose, and community relevance.

What We Prioritize

Underrepresented or rural creatives

Projects rooted in African identity, language, and culture

Youth-led or women-led artistic initiatives

Creative works addressing social justice, mental health, climate, or education

High-potential creatives lacking access to formal opportunities

What We Do Not Fund

  • • Commercial projects not linked to creative development or impact
  • • Projects without original creative work or cultural relevance
  • • Ongoing salaries or personal expenses
  • • Activities that are already completed or fully funded elsewhere

How to Apply

Applications will soon open on our website via a dedicated Creative Arts Portal. Prepare these requirements:

1

Project Proposal

A detailed project proposal or creative concept

2

Portfolio Sample

Short portfolio or sample of past work (video, image, sound, or document)

3

Budget Breakdown

A budget breakdown of the grant amount requested

4

Impact Statement

A statement of impact (why this project matters)

5

Documentation

Proof of nationality and short artist bio

Review Process

All applications will be reviewed by our panel of African creatives, cultural leaders, and industry mentors to ensure fair and expert evaluation.
