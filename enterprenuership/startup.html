From Research to Reality

The Startup Support Fund by Reuben Foundation Africa is designed to turn research-backed ideas from young Africans into fully functional, impactful businesses. We believe the future of African development lies in homegrown innovation â€” and it starts with the research being conducted by African students today.

Many final-year students and graduates complete valuable research projects with real-world potential, yet these innovations often end up archived or unpublished. Our Startup Support Fund ensures that these ideas are not wasted, but transformed into viable enterprises that create jobs, generate income, and solve pressing challenges across the continent.

EXCLUSIVE

A Direct Pathway: From Research to Business

This fund is uniquely linked to our Research Fund, and only accepts applications from researchers who have conducted their projects under our supervision. Once the research is completed, the researcher can submit their final research report directly through our research portal for evaluation and support under the Startup Support Fund.

Important: There is no separate application for funding. Once a supervised research project is completed and submitted, we initiate the commercialization process.

What the Startup Support Fund Offers

Business Conversion & Setup Support

We guide the researcher through transforming their findings into a real business by helping with:

  • Business model design based on the research
  • Market validation and customer discovery
  • Business registration and legal compliance
  • Product or prototype development
  • Go-to-market strategy

Seed Capital (Non-Loan)

$1,000 - $10,000

We provide equity-free startup funding covering:

  • Equipment and tools
  • Licensing and regulatory permits
  • MVP (Minimum Viable Product) testing
  • Branding and digital setup
  • Launch activities

Mentorship and Coaching

Each innovator is matched with sector-specific mentors to support their growth journey. Mentorship covers:

  • Strategic planning and startup leadership
  • Operations and supply chain design
  • Pitching and investor preparation
  • Legal, compliance, and scaling strategies

Training, Bootcamps & Community Access

We enroll all supported innovators in our Startup Launch School, a practical crash course that includes:

  • Startup strategy and execution
  • Financial literacy and investment readiness
  • Social impact and business ethics
  • Grant writing and fundraising
  • Digital marketing and sales systems

Join our Africa Startup Fellows Network, a growing community of African entrepreneurs supported by the Foundation.

Who Is Eligible?

To qualify, you must:

Be a citizen of any African country

Must be a resident or citizen of any African nation

Have completed a research project under our Research Fund program

Only supervised research participants are eligible

Submit your final research report via our online Research Portal

Complete research documentation required

Show clear potential for the project to be commercialized

Research must have viable business applications

Be willing to participate in our mentorship and training process

Commitment to the full support program required

Important: Only supervised researchers from our affiliated universities or research funding program are eligible. No external applications are accepted.

How the Process Works

Research Phase

You conduct research under our supervision (via our Research Fund program)

Submission

You upload your final report and commercialization summary to our Research Portal

Evaluation

Our team reviews the research to assess business potential

Invitation

If selected, you are invited into our Startup Support Program

Support

We begin funding, mentorship, and business development immediately

What We Prioritize

We prioritize ideas that:

Address real challenges in Africa (health, agriculture, education, climate, technology, etc.)
Have potential for job creation and income generation
Show innovation, feasibility, and scalability
Can be launched within 3 to 6 months of support

What We Do Not Fund

Projects not backed by completed research
Unsupervised research or unverified concepts
Startups unrelated to the submitted research
Ideas seeking funding for personal or unrelated business purposes
