Reuben Foundation Africa Student Research and Development Fund

Popularly known as the “Student Research Allowance”

Overview

The Reuben Foundation Africa Student Research and Development Fund, popularly referred to as the Student Research Allowance, is a flagship initiative designed to support final-year undergraduate and postgraduate students across Africa undertaking high-impact research projects. The primary goal is to enable students to complete their research, overcome financial barriers, and contribute knowledge to areas crucial to Africa’s development.

Initially, the program is available to students in Ghanaian tertiary institutions only, with plans to expand to other African countries soon. The fund addresses financial and logistical challenges students face while conducting meaningful academic research, supporting innovative projects that solve Africa’s developmental issues and advance global knowledge.

Purpose of the Fund

  • Support Quality Research: Original, student-led research aligned with national and global development goals.
  • Remove Financial Barriers: Eases the financial burden of fieldwork, data collection, equipment, and other essential research activities.
  • Promote Innovation: Encourages a culture of innovation and relevance, motivating students to explore impactful, community-focused research.
  • Strengthen Educational Capacity: Helps tertiary institutions nurture skilled graduates prepared to lead in various sectors across Africa and globally.

Who Will Be Eligible to Apply?

Open to final-year undergraduate and postgraduate students enrolled in accredited tertiary institutions in Africa, including:

  • Public and private universities
  • Nursing training colleges
  • Teacher training colleges (Colleges of Education)
  • Polytechnics and technical universities

Initially, only Ghanaian students (including foreign nationals studying in Ghana) are eligible. The fund will later be extended to other African countries.

Eligibility Requirements:

  • Final year undergraduate or postgraduate studies
  • Research project required for graduation
  • Demonstrated financial need
  • Research topics endorsed by an academic supervisor and aligned with the fund’s objectives

Eligible Research Areas

  • Health & Public Health
  • Nursing, Midwifery, and Clinical Research
  • Education & Teaching Methodology
  • Agriculture & Food Security
  • Climate & Environmental Sustainability
  • Digital Technology & Innovation (AI, Blockchain, IoT, etc.)
  • Governance, Peace & Development
  • Business, Finance & Economic Research
  • Social Justice, Gender Equality & Inclusion
  • Science, Engineering & Applied Fields

What Will the Allowance Cover?

  • Fieldwork and travel
  • Internet and data collection tools
  • Laboratory testing and equipment
  • Research materials, printing, and binding
  • Software, surveys, and analysis tools
  • Communication and transport related to research

Each grant will be tailored to project needs, with funding capped based on the scope.

How Will Students Apply?

  1. Create an account on the Reuben Foundation Africa platform.
  2. Submit the following documents:
    • Research proposal (max 5 pages)
    • Academic supervisor’s endorsement letter
    • Transcript or proof of enrollment
    • National/student ID
    • Detailed budget breakdown
    • Optional: CV or portfolio

Selection Criteria

  • Relevance and originality of the research
  • Clarity and feasibility of methodology
  • Potential for impact
  • Academic standing and supervisor endorsement
  • Budget justification

Disbursement & Reporting

  • Funds disbursed in two phases: 70% upon approval, 30% upon submission of the final report
  • Recipients must submit:
    • Midway progress report
    • Final thesis or research report
    • 1-page summary of findings
    • Optional: Short video reflection/testimonial

Application Timeline

  • Applications Open: Early January each year
  • Deadline: June ending
  • Disbursement: Following approval
