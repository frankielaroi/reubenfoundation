Fighting for Freedom. Restoring Dignity. Reforming Systems.

Our Mission

Every year, innocent people across Africa are arrested, tried, and imprisoned for crimes they did not commit. Some are victims of flawed investigations, coerced confessions, false accusations, or legal systems that fail the poor and voiceless.

Many spend years, sometimes decades behind bars with no access to fair legal representation or a chance to prove their innocence.

The Justice & Exoneration subprogram exists to uncover these miscarriages of justice, advocate for the wrongfully imprisoned, and restore hope and dignity to individuals and families affected by unjust incarceration.

We Work To:

Free the innocent
Support their reintegration
Push for legal reforms

What We Do Not Support

To maintain focus and integrity, we do not take on cases that meet the following conditions:

  • Cases already receiving full legal funding or team support
  • Criminal cases involving violence or terrorism (unless strong proof of wrongful arrest)
  • Politically motivated cases without clear legal foundation
  • Civil disputes involving business, land, or inheritance conflicts

We prioritize criminal justice matters involving clear indications of wrongful conviction and lack of access to justice.
